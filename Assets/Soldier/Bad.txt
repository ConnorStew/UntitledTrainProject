You hand the coin out to the soldier explaining the next stop is for him. He accepts it and as the train slows, to a stop the doors open.
With a hand on his pistol, he charges out onto the platform with a powerful cry that stops suddenly.
The train waits a moment and you see the soldier step back onto the train. Helmet and pistol gone as he approaches you. He solemnly tells you his platoon only lasted two weeks. He thanks you anyway,  saying he tried his  best.
He walks away clutching at a bottle of bourbon from the overhead, going deeper into the train. You feel a wave of sadness envelop you
